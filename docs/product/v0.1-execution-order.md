## Track 2: Self-Judged Mechanism Validation (current phase)

Defined 2026-07-10. This is the active phase while no third-party tester is available.

### Two separate validation questions

v0.1 success splits into two independent questions that must not share one gate:

1. Mechanism validity: on real material, does the evidence-backed review flow catch real errors, at what recall and precision, and how long does one pass take? This is judged by measured data. It does not require a third party; a third party's only unique contribution here is material, and self-sourced or authorized real material substitutes for it.
2. Adoption willingness: does someone with the pain want this? This genuinely requires the target cohort and is deliberately deferred to the Lead A session. Self-testing must never be claimed to answer it.

Gating question 1 on a third party was rejected as an unnecessary external dependency on an objectively measurable question.

### Pilot procedure (no new product code)

1. Obtain one real ASR draft of self-owned or authorized content, produced outside the product (for example an exported auto-caption track). The material must predate this plan; do not record new material for the test.
2. Hand-correct the draft to a final version. The diff between draft and final is the ground truth: every real error, categorized as enumerable term / homophone requiring context / colloquial rephrasing / segmentation / timing / other. Each correction is also marked "would I have made this correction anyway", to separate real fixes from cosmetic ones.
3. Build a glossary for the material up front, run the full review flow, and measure detector performance against the ground truth.

### Pass criteria (all required)

- End-to-end run on the real material completes: input SRT to reviewed SRT plus decision log, no crash, output consistent with MD-003 semantics.
- Detector recall on the enumerable-term error class is at least 80%, with the glossary built before the run. A miss caused by a term absent from the glossary is a glossary-coverage gap, not a mechanism failure; a miss on a term the glossary correctly lists is a mechanism failure.
- Precision of surfaced ReviewCases is at least 60% true positives. Below that, review burden exceeds review value.
- Average handling time per decision is recorded. This run establishes the baseline; no threshold yet.

### Failure criteria (any one triggers direction review before further feature work)

- Enumerable-term errors are under 20% of all real errors in the ground truth. The glossary-driven interception ceiling is then too low, and redirection matters more than any new feature.
- The matching mechanism misses most targets that the glossary correctly lists.

The 80% / 60% / 20% thresholds are agreed defaults as of 2026-07-10; adjust only with an explicit note here.

### Labeling rule

A passing result is stated as "the mechanism works on real material", nothing more. It is not product validation and not evidence that anyone wants the product. This wording restriction is what makes the self-judged gate honest enough to present to Lead A later.

### Code-change rules during this phase

No new product slices. Permitted code changes are limited to:

- defects found while running the pilot, reproduced by a test before being fixed;
- swapping the built-in demo glossary content for a session or pilot glossary (no glossary file format, no config system).

If the pilot suggests new detector capabilities, record the finding in a discussion document. Building it is a decision for after Lead A material arrives.

## Third-Party Test Readiness

Internal work continues in small bounded slices, but externally there is exactly one consolidated test event with Lead A. Individual slices are not separately shown to or tested with third parties; the first impression is spent once.

"Prepared enough to approach Lead A" is defined by this checklist, and only by it. All items checked means schedule the session; any item unchecked means not yet. The checklist exists in both directions: it prevents approaching unprepared, and it prevents indefinite polishing by giving preparation a defined endpoint.

1. Mechanism proven: Track 2 pass criteria met on real material.
2. Data to show: a one-page pilot result ("on one real ASR draft, the flow surfaced M of N enumerable-term errors").
3. Lead A's cost minimized and stated in one sentence: one or two episodes' ASR draft plus final subtitles, and one session of a measured length taken from the dry run, not guessed.
4. Session rehearsed: the full facilitated flow has been run at least once end to end (Track 2 doubles as the rehearsal); script, record sheet, and the three session metrics are final.
5. Lead A's glossary pre-built from their public content, so the session demonstrates their errors being caught, not a generic demo.
6. Data-handling statement ready: what the material is used for, where it is stored, that it does not leave the machine.

## Validation Criteria For The Lead A Session

Measure three things:

1. True positives: how many real errors in one episode the glossary detector surfaces, using a channel glossary built with Lead A.
2. Time: Lead A's normal correction time for one episode versus the time through this flow.
3. Vision reaction: whether the decision log and reviewed SRT read as valuable to Lead A, or as noise. This tests the flywheel hypothesis, not just the detector.

If the detector finds nearly nothing on real material, that is a result, not a failure of the session: it redirects detector priorities before more detector code is written.
